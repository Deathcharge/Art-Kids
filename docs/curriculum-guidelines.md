# Art Kids Curriculum Guidelines

## Curriculum Philosophy

The Art Kids curriculum is designed to provide comprehensive, age-appropriate art education that develops technical skills, creative thinking, and cultural awareness. Our approach balances:

- **Skill Development**: Building foundational and advanced art techniques
- **Creative Expression**: Encouraging individual voice and artistic exploration
- **Cultural Literacy**: Understanding art history and diverse artistic traditions
- **Critical Thinking**: Analyzing, interpreting, and discussing art
- **Joy and Engagement**: Making art learning fun and meaningful

## Curriculum Framework

### Core Components

Every Art Kids program includes:

1. **Studio Practice** (60% of time)
   - Hands-on art making
   - Exploration of various media
   - Technical skill building
   - Creative experimentation

2. **Art Appreciation** (20% of time)
   - Viewing and discussing artworks
   - Learning about artists and art movements
   - Understanding cultural contexts
   - Developing visual literacy

3. **Reflection and Critique** (10% of time)
   - Discussing own work and peers' work
   - Developing vocabulary for art discussion
   - Building self-assessment skills
   - Celebrating achievements

4. **Connection and Application** (10% of time)
   - Relating art to other subjects
   - Connecting to students' lives and communities
   - Understanding art careers and applications
   - Planning future projects

## Learning Domains

### 1. Creating

**Students will:**
- Generate and conceptualize artistic ideas
- Organize and develop artistic work
- Refine and complete artistic work
- Present, share, and showcase artwork

### 2. Presenting

**Students will:**
- Select and prepare work for presentation
- Develop and refine artistic displays
- Convey meaning through presentation
- Share work with various audiences

### 3. Responding

**Students will:**
- Perceive and analyze artistic work
- Interpret intent and meaning in art
- Apply criteria to evaluate art
- Develop preferences and justify opinions

### 4. Connecting

**Students will:**
- Synthesize art knowledge with other disciplines
- Relate art to personal, societal, cultural, and historical contexts
- Understand how art shapes and reflects society
- Explore art careers and applications

## Age-Appropriate Programming

### Early Elementary (Grades K-2)

**Focus**: Exploration, Experimentation, Fun

**Skills:**
- Basic use of art materials (crayons, markers, paint, scissors, glue)
- Color recognition and mixing
- Simple shapes and patterns
- Fine motor development
- Self-expression through art

**Typical Projects:**
- Painting and drawing with various tools
- Simple collages and assemblages
- Introduction to clay and modeling
- Printmaking basics
- Found object art

**Art Appreciation:**
- Exposure to colorful, engaging artworks
- Simple discussions about what they see
- Connecting art to stories and experiences
- Introduction to famous children's book illustrators and artists

### Upper Elementary (Grades 3-5)

**Focus**: Skill Building, Technique, Cultural Awareness

**Skills:**
- Refined drawing techniques (proportion, perspective basics)
- Advanced color theory
- Various painting methods
- Sculpture and 3D construction
- Design principles (balance, contrast, emphasis)

**Typical Projects:**
- Observational drawing
- Landscape and still life painting
- Sculpture with multiple materials
- Fiber arts and weaving
- Mixed media artwork

**Art Appreciation:**
- Introduction to art history and major movements
- Learning about diverse artists and cultures
- Discussing meaning and interpretation
- Making connections between artworks

### Middle School (Grades 6-8)

**Focus**: Refinement, Expression, Critical Thinking

**Skills:**
- Advanced drawing (shading, perspective, figure drawing)
- Sophisticated use of color and composition
- Complex sculptural techniques
- Digital art introduction
- Portfolio development

**Typical Projects:**
- Personal narrative artwork
- Social issue art and advocacy
- Portraits and self-portraits
- Installation and conceptual art
- Collaborative murals or large-scale works

**Art Appreciation:**
- In-depth study of art movements and history
- Contemporary art and current artists
- Critique and art writing
- Understanding art's role in society

### High School (Grades 9-12)

**Focus**: Mastery, Portfolio, Preparation

**Skills:**
- Mastery of chosen media
- Personal style development
- Advanced techniques across multiple disciplines
- Art career preparation
- Portfolio curation

**Typical Projects:**
- Self-directed inquiry projects
- Advanced studio work in chosen concentration
- Community-engaged art projects
- Exhibition-ready portfolio pieces
- Cross-disciplinary collaborations

**Art Appreciation:**
- Advanced art history and theory
- Critical analysis and writing
- Understanding art markets and careers
- Contemporary art practices

## Media and Techniques Coverage

### Drawing
- Pencil, charcoal, pen and ink
- Pastels and oil pastels
- Colored pencils
- Sketching and observational drawing

### Painting
- Watercolor
- Tempera and acrylic
- Mixed media painting
- Various painting surfaces

### Sculpture and 3D
- Clay (pottery and sculpture)
- Paper sculpture
- Found object assemblage
- Wire and soft sculpture

### Printmaking
- Relief printing (stamps, linoleum)
- Monoprinting
- Collograph
- Screen printing (advanced)

### Collage and Mixed Media
- Paper collage
- Fabric and fiber arts
- Assemblage
- Combination techniques

### Digital Arts (when resources available)
- Digital drawing and painting
- Photo editing and manipulation
- Graphic design basics
- Digital portfolios

## Cultural and Historical Content

Art Kids curriculum includes diverse representation:

- **Global Perspectives**: Art from various cultures and continents
- **Historical Range**: Ancient to contemporary art
- **Diverse Artists**: Including women artists, artists of color, LGBTQ+ artists, artists with disabilities
- **Traditional and Contemporary**: Both historical techniques and current practices
- **Local and Global**: Community artists and international art

## Alignment with Standards

Art Kids curriculum aligns with:
- National Visual Arts Standards
- State arts education frameworks
- 21st Century Skills (creativity, collaboration, critical thinking, communication)
- Social-emotional learning competencies

## Assessment

Student progress is assessed through:

- **Formative Assessment**: Ongoing observation and feedback during art making
- **Self-Assessment**: Student reflection on their own work and growth
- **Peer Discussion**: Collaborative critique and feedback
- **Portfolio Development**: Collection of work showing progress over time
- **Project Completion**: Successful completion of artworks
- **Participation**: Engagement in studio practice and discussion

## Differentiation

Art Kids curriculum is differentiated to meet diverse learner needs:

- **Multiple entry points**: Projects accessible at various skill levels
- **Choice and voice**: Options for subject matter, media, and approach
- **Scaffolding**: Support structures for students who need them
- **Extension opportunities**: Challenges for advanced students
- **Various learning modalities**: Visual, hands-on, verbal, and collaborative learning
- **Cultural responsiveness**: Connecting to students' backgrounds and experiences

## Materials and Resources

Each unit includes:
- Detailed lesson plans

## Curriculum Development

The Art Kids curriculum is continuously refined through:
- Educator feedback and experience
- Student response and outcomes
- Current research in arts education
- Evolving art practices and technologies
- Partner school input
- Cultural and community relevance
